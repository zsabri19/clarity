The 5 Signs

Rate each one honestly. If any resonate, you already know where to start.

01

Decision Latency

How long does it take between a decision being made and action being taken? If the gap exceeds 48 hours for anything under $100K, clarity is not installed.

02

Role Ambiguity

Ask any three people in your org: "Who owns X?" If you get three different answers, you do not have an execution problem. You have a clarity problem.

03

Signal Overload

How many priorities does each person carry? More than three means nothing is actually a priority. Signal overload is the first symptom of a missing decision architecture.

04

Recurring Firefighting

The same crisis surfaces every quarter. Same type of fire. Same cast of heroes. That is not bad luck. That is a structural gap masked as urgency.

05

Authority Confusion

When pressure hits, who decides? If the answer changes depending on who is in the room, your governance is not real. Authority confusion is the number one cause of transformation stalling.

One or more of these feel familiar?
